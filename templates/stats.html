How We Calculate This

Waste percentages are derived from EPA, USDA, and company sustainability reports. "Families fed" uses USDA data for a family of 4 — 5.5 lbs/day · 38 lbs/week · 2,000 lbs/year. We divide documented waste by 38 lbs to calculate weekly family equivalents.

We always round down. We never inflate. If the math doesn't produce a whole family, we don't count it. This methodology is public and locked. It cannot be changed without founder review.

Subsidy data is sourced from USDA procurement records, EWG Farm Subsidy Database, and public SEC filings. All sources are public record.

Sources — All Public Record

Waste percentages: EPA Food Recovery Hierarchy, USDA Economic Research Service, company sustainability reports and SEC filings.

Subsidy data: USDA Agricultural Marketing Service procurement records, Environmental Working Group Farm Subsidy Database, company 10-K filings.

Executive data: Company press releases, SEC regulatory filings, public news coverage. Verified April 2026. Updated when leadership changes.

CEO pay ratios: SEC DEF 14A proxy statements · AFL-CIO Executive Paywatch · Source: public record, required disclosure since 2018.

SNAP sales data: Numerator Research 2024 consumer panel data. SNAP = Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program (food stamps) — federal taxpayer-funded food assistance.

Subsidy data explained: Federal farm subsidies flow to commodity crop farmers (corn, soy, wheat) whose crops are primary ingredients for these corporations, artificially lowering their raw material costs at taxpayer expense.

Retailer data: SEC proxy statements, GAO reports, OpenSecrets.org, USDA SNAP program data, Good Jobs First subsidy tracker. All public record.
